How much money can I get from returning an eligible beverage container?

States offering 5¢ per container: California (under 24 oz), Guam, Hawaii, Iowa, Maine (under 50 mL), Massachusetts, New York, Vermont (all non-liquor containers) States offering 10¢ per container: California (over 24 oz), Connecticut, Michigan, Oregon Alcohol/Liquor containers:

  • California: 25¢ for wine and distilled spirits in a box, bladder, or pouch
  • Maine: 15¢ for all wine and liquor containers over 50 mL
  • Vermont: 15¢ for all liquor containers

How do the Bottle Bill refundable deposits work?

A small deposit is added to the cost of a beverage when a retailer purchases it from a distributor. When buying such a beverage, the consumer (you!) then pays that deposit to the retailer. By returning the empty beverage container to the retail store or a redemption center, the deposit is refunded to the consumer. Note: Most U.S. states have an additional handling fee to cover the cost of handling the containers.

What's the handling fee?

Ranging from 0.007¢ to 9.7¢, the handling fee varies greatly by state. Click on your state up above to find out your handling fee!
